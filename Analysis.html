Analysis of "Yellow or Red"

“Yellow or Red” explores the conflict in Thailand between the People’s Alliance for Democracy (PAD) and the People’s Power Party (PPP), which emerged from the turmoil of 2005 and 2006. Supporters of the PAD wore yellow shirts to show their loyalty to King Bhumibol Adulyadej, while the red-shirted followers of the UDD, or Democracy Against Dictatorship, backed the ousted Prime Minister Thaksin Shinawatra after his removal in a military coup in September 2006. In this poem, the speaker uses these two colors as a metaphor for identity, asserting that he isn’t just yellow or red, or even a mix of the two—he is every color at once, reflecting the complexity of human experience and emotion..

Growing up in this culturally rich environment, he draws inspiration from his surroundings and personal experiences to delve into themes of identity and the emotional struggles many face in society.

His poetry is marked by vivid imagery and thoughtful use of color symbolism, making his explorations of joy and sorrow feel relatable and profound. Theme

The central theme of "Yellow or Red" revolves around duality and choice. It delves into the complexities of human emotions, illustrating how contrasting feelings can coexist within a single person The poem explores themes of identity, the struggle for self-acceptance, and the quest for clarity amidst confusion. The interplay of colors serves as a metaphor for the myriad emotions we experience and the choices we make in response to them..

Mood

The mood of the poem oscillates between introspection and tension. At times, it conveys a sense of yearning and hope associated with yellow, while at other moments, it reflects the intensity and turmoil associated with red. This shifting mood captures the essence of the speaker's emotional landscape, highlighting the struggle to navigate between these contrasting feelings..
